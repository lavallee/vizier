title: State the headline claim before building
type: principle
---

Write the one sentence a reader should leave with, before you've drawn
a chart. Then build backwards from it.

Example: `soma-demand-signals` started with "SOMA is unusual in these
specific ways compared to peers" as the headline, then built the chart
that showed it. The alternative — build an interesting chart, *then*
find a headline — routinely produces charts that bury or contradict
the lede.

If the headline claim turns out to be wrong once the data's loaded,
rewrite it. The discipline is *having* one at every moment, not having
the right one at the start.
