title: Generate variants; select after
type: principle
---

At key decision points (frame, chart form, headline, color scheme,
ordering) produce 2–4 variants rather than one. Selection goes through
concrete criteria — reader test, critique record, judge persona — not
gut preference.

The load-bearing implication: **each pass has to be cheap**. If a single
sketch costs a day you won't generate three, and you'll over-commit to
the first idea. This turns shared scaffolding, fast data re-bind, and
template sketches into engineering *requirements*, not nice-to-haves.
The goal isn't more options for their own sake — it's that options-then-
select produces better work than one-shot attempts, because choice
exposes assumptions that solo drafting hides.
