title: Editorial hooks surface the counter-narrative at the chart
type: principle
---

The Frame-stage principle *name the counter-narrative you want to
rule out* says you should know, before building, what the obvious
alternative reading is. Its Narrate-stage expression is that readers
should see it too — on or near the chart, in short cards or hooks
that say "you might think X; here's why we don't show that."

Example: `new-bern-profile/elections.js` pairs the turnout-per-election
small multiples with six editorial hooks naming specific gotchas —
"the denominator is today's active voters," "the voter file tells you
who's registered, not how they voted," "turnout is concentrated at
the top of the age ladder." These hooks do what the chart form alone
can't: they name the misreadings the chart is *designed* to prevent,
turning chart-form discipline into explicit reader-facing trust.

Skipping this step means the chart silently rules out a reading the
reader never knew was on the table. Naming it gives the reader credit —
they can see the care, not just receive it.
