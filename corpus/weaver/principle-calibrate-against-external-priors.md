title: Calibrate against external priors
type: principle
---

If your simulation's output contradicts well-established research, the
default assumption should be that your simulation is wrong.

Our first pass had Cupertino's down-ballot "informed" rate at 22% —
above the research prior ceiling of 5–15%. The fix was tightening the
evaluative-gate parameters and adding a down-ballot substance cap. We
could have published the original number (it looked "right" to
researchers-are-biased intuition), but it would have been indefensible.

Build calibration checks into the pipeline, not just the final
validation.
