title: What the data can and can't say
type: principle
---

Before any framing, enumerate:

- **Claims the data supports.** Concretely — "we can show turnout per
  election × age band" — not abstractly.
- **Claims it can't support but a reader might assume.** The gotchas.
  A chart showing turnout over time with today's voter file as
  denominator cannot support "young-voter turnout dropped" — the
  denominator biases older elections down.
- **Claims it could support with additional ingest.** What would it take
  to answer the question the data almost answers?

This page prevents Frame from committing to a headline the data can't
bear. It's also the honest version of "what's interesting here" — you
can't know that without first knowing what the data can say at all.
