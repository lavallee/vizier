title: Declare the audience and their prior knowledge
type: principle
---

Write one line: *this reader knows X, does not know Y, cares about Z.*
Choices about jargon, glossary depth, and headline framing all follow
from it.

The same sankey written for academic public-admin readers, civic
funders, and local journalists needs three different glossaries and
three different ledes. Declaring the audience once at the top keeps
you from trying to serve all three and serving none. Pairs with
*plain-language labels* in Narrate — that principle tells you how,
this one tells you *for whom*.
