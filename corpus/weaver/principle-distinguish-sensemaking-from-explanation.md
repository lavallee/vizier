title: Distinguish sensemaking from explanation
type: principle
---

Most weaver projects start as **sensemaking** — we use the viz to
understand the data ourselves — and only some graduate to **explanation**
for an outside audience. These have different rules.

- **Sensemaking**: optimize for iteration speed. Rough labels, no glossary,
  exploratory interactions are fine. Over-polish here is waste.
- **Explanation**: optimize for cold-reader comprehension. Every
  Narrate/Ship principle applies. Interactions are earned, not default.

Most projects cross the line partway through. Name the moment — that's
when the ship checklist kicks in, not before. Marking a project as
sensemaking keeps you from applying ship-grade polish to a chart you'll
throw away; marking it as explanation keeps you from publishing a
sensemaking sketch as if it were finished.
